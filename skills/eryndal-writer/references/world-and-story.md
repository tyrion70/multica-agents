# Eryndal World & Story Reference

Condensed reference for writing prose. For full detail, consult the project PDFs in `/mnt/project/`.

## The World in Brief

**Valdris** — single large continent. 73 years ago, the Waking shattered an 800-year multi-racial federation called the Concord. The Concord was governed by the Accord (legal charter), powered by the Resonance Lines (elven magical infrastructure), and connected by the Deep Roads (dwarven tunnels). All three are gone.

**The Waking** — Dwarven miners at Khorrund Vel broke into a cavity that shouldn't have existed. Something beneath the world — the Sleeper — moved. Not an attack. Not malice. Something vast and alien simply stirred, and civilisation broke around it like insects on a shifting mattress.

**The Broken Age** — the present. Roughly a dozen successor states. The grandparents who survived the Waking are dying. Their grandchildren have never known the Concord. The tremors haven't stopped.

## The Five Regions

| Region | Character | Key Detail |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **The Ironspine** | Central mountains, dwarven settlements | Wound Sites densest here. Passes watched by Undermount Clans. Peaks emit tones at night. |
| **The Vael** | NW ancient forest, elven territory | Treeline = political border. Vael'Sorn closed 40 years. The Stillwood (Wound Site) here. |
| **The Hearthlands** | S/SW plains and rivers, human powers | Hegemony dominates centre/east. Freeholds on western coast. Concordance Ruins in disputed middle. |
| **The Drowned Reach** | Redrawn eastern coast | Three cities drowned (Verath, Solan's Reach, Caldenmere). Tidal wetlands, stilted salvage towns. Caldenmere's harbour gate moves. |
| **The Ashfields** | NE badlands | Grey soil, permanent haze, wrong-coloured rain. Three Wound Sites. Wound Holds most numerous here. Dara's homeland. |

## The Five Factions

| Faction | Nature | Key Tension |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Hegemony of Arath** | Largest human state. Bureaucratic, quietly brutal. Building an empire. | Believes the Concord failed from too much democracy. Controls licensed magic. |
| **The Freeholds** | Loose city-state confederation. Idealistic, weak, fractious. | Trying to preserve Accord values with decreasing success. Philosophically right, practically failing. |
| **Undermount Clans** | Surface-only dwarves. Clan-based, no central government. | Carry collective guilt for the Waking. Will not go underground. Maintain the seals on the Deep Roads. |
| **Silven Retreat** | Elves withdrawn into the Vael. | Knew more than they admitted before the Waking. Know more now. Withdrawal is partly guilt. |
| **Wound Holds** | Communities around the 11 Wound Sites. | Closest to the Sleeper. Some worship it. Some study it. Considered dangerous by everyone else. |

## The Racial Fractures

Everyone blames everyone. All parties have a partial point. No one is cleanly right.
- **Humans blame dwarves** for drilling too deep.
- **Dwarves blame elven mages** for failing to prevent the Resonance Collapse.
- **Elves blame human political pressure** for overriding ancient dwarven drilling prohibitions.

## Magic — Quick Reference

**Two layers:** The Resonance (surface layer, now broken) was a translation system built atop the Source (deep layer, still flowing). The Resonance Lines channelled the Source safely. Without them, the Source reaches the surface raw — powerful and dangerous.

**The secret:** The Sleeper *permitted* the Resonance to function. Permission was withdrawn because civilisation's use of power turned from collective good to self-interest. The Waking was a consequence, not a punishment. Magic can return — if civilisation earns it back. Nobody understands this yet except fragments in Serevane's notes.

**The Drev exception:** The Drev's connection to the Source was never through the Resonance Lines. It was never revoked. They are the only people who still have full, unbroken permission. Nobody knows this. The locked archive in Vael'Sorn contains the evidence. The elves sealed it 40 years ago.

## The Creatures

| Creature | Nature | Key Rule for Prose |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **The Pale** | Source-hunter. Heat-haze in human shape. Arrives, stands still, then is closer. Kills by closing a practitioner's attunement permanently. | **Ignores the Drev entirely.** Write this so it's missable on first read. |
| **The Veth / The Mourne** | Ancient instruments of consequence. Kill the corrupt — no wound, body simply stops. | The terror is the aftermath: everyone starts asking what the dead person did. Write the communal reckoning, not the kill. |

## Series Seeds Planted in Book One

These are threads that must be present but not resolved:
- **Serevane's missing final pages** — removed before she hid the box. In the sub-chamber with the Drev door.
- **The locked archive in Vael'Sorn** — engine of Book Two.
- **The Drev door in the ruins** — Tam stood at it, didn't enter. Predates the Accord.
- **Dara witnessing the impossible thing** — she felt the Source-silence spike. She hasn't spoken yet.
- **Halric learning properly** — the elder knows things from oral tradition, including something about the Drev.
- **Increasing tremors** — Halric has been counting. They're accelerating.
- **The Hegemony's intelligence source** — someone knew the group's route. Leak or magical surveillance.
- **The Freehold agents** — found something else in the ruins. Gone before the group left.
