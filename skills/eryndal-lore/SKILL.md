# Eryndal Lore & Canon

Condensed canon for **Eryndal** — the world, factions, magic, characters, and
creatures of Valdris in the Broken Age. Use this whenever a task needs world
facts, faction behaviour, the magic rules, or character truth across ANY pillar
(book, card game, art, RPG, social). This is a working reference, not the master:
the full **World Bible** (`Bible/Eryndal_World_Bible_Vol1–6`) and
`continuity/tracker.md` in the repo are authoritative — read them for depth and
NEVER invent lore from memory. For prose craft use `eryndal-writer`; for the
project map use `eryndal-universe`.

## The world in brief
**Valdris** — one large continent. 73 years ago the **Waking** shattered an
800-year multi-racial federation, the **Concord** (governed by the Accord,
powered by elven **Resonance Lines**, connected by dwarven **Deep Roads** — all
gone). Dwarven miners at Khorrund Vel broke into a cavity that shouldn't have
existed and something vast beneath the world — the **Sleeper** — moved. Not
malice; it simply stirred, and civilisation broke around it. The present is the
**Broken Age**: ~a dozen successor states, the survivors dying off, tremors
ongoing.

## Five regions
- **The Ironspine** — central mountains, dwarven settlements; densest Wound Sites; peaks emit tones at night.
- **The Vael** — NW ancient forest, elven territory; Vael'Sorn closed 40 years; the Stillwood Wound Site.
- **The Hearthlands** — S/SW plains, human powers; Hegemony dominant; Concordance Ruins in the disputed middle.
- **The Drowned Reach** — redrawn eastern coast; three drowned cities; stilted salvage towns.
- **The Ashfields** — NE badlands; grey soil, permanent haze, wrong-coloured rain; three Wound Sites; Dara's homeland.

## Five factions
- **Hegemony of Arath** — largest human state; bureaucratic, quietly brutal, empire-building; licenses magic; thinks the Concord failed from too much democracy.
- **The Freeholds** — loose, idealistic, fractious city-states; preserving Accord values and losing; philosophically right, practically failing.
- **Undermount Clans** — surface-only dwarves; carry collective guilt for the Waking; won't go underground; maintain the seals on the Deep Roads.
- **Silven Retreat** — elves withdrawn into the Vael; knew more than they admitted, know more now; withdrawal is partly guilt.
- **Wound Holds** — communities around the 11 Wound Sites; closest to the Sleeper; some worship, some study; distrusted by all.

**The racial fractures:** humans blame dwarves for drilling; dwarves blame elven
mages for the Resonance Collapse; elves blame human political pressure. Everyone
has a partial point; no one is cleanly right.

## Magic — and the central secret
Two layers: the **Resonance** (surface translation layer, now broken) sat atop the
**Source** (deep layer, still flowing). The Lines channelled the Source safely;
without them it reaches the surface raw — powerful and dangerous.

**The secret (almost nobody knows):** the Sleeper *permitted* the Resonance to
function. Permission was withdrawn when civilisation's use of power turned from
collective good to self-interest. The Waking was a *consequence*, not a
punishment. Magic can return if civilisation earns it back. **The Drev exception:**
the Drev's connection to the Source never ran through the Resonance Lines, so it
was never revoked — they alone still have full permission, and nobody knows.
Evidence is in the locked archive in Vael'Sorn (sealed 40 years ago) and in
Serevane's notes.

## Five protagonists (at a glance — full voice guides in `eryndal-writer`)
- **Aldric Crane** — human, 34, Hegemony captain, licensed Resonance. Controlled, observational, loyal until the cracks show.
- **Tam Ashwell** — Drev, 67, cookhouse owner. Warm, attentive; does impossible things without knowing it; the structural deception of the series.
- **Halric Keln** — Undermount dwarf, 31, craftsman, self-taught Stonewhisper. Blunt, physical; grief expressed through stonework.
- **Seris** — Silven elf, ~180, former scholar, diminished Resonance. Precise, guarded; eyes shift colour with emotion; knows what Tam is.
- **Dara** — human, 24, Wound Hold practitioner from the Ashfields. Pragmatic, direct; changing in ways she hasn't noticed.

## Creatures (handle with care)
- **The Pale** — Source-hunter; heat-haze in human shape; kills practitioners by closing their attunement permanently. **Ignores the Drev entirely** (write it missable).
- **The Veth / The Mourne** — ancient instruments of consequence; kill the corrupt with no wound, the body simply stops. The horror is the communal reckoning afterward.

## Canon guardrails
- The World Bible and `continuity/tracker.md` win on every fact. When unsure, read them or escalate to the human / Loremaster — do not guess.
- The series' three **cardinal writing rules** are inviolable in prose: no lore
  dumps, no theme-aware characters, stop before you explain. (See `eryndal-writer`.)
- Keep canon consistent across pillars — a card's flavour text, a portrait, a
  social post, and the prose must not contradict the Bible or each other.
- Book One series seeds that must stay unresolved: Serevane's missing final pages;
  the locked Vael'Sorn archive; the Drev door in the ruins; Dara having felt the
  Source-silence; Halric learning Stonewhisper properly; accelerating tremors; the
  Hegemony's intelligence leak; the Freehold agents' unknown find.
